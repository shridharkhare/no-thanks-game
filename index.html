No Thanks!

How to play

How to play

No Thanks! is a card game designed to be as simple as possible to learn while still being fun to play. Players must employ strategic Decision-making and skillful Risk Management. Evaluating the value of each card, understanding when to take risks and when to play it safe, and making astute choices are crucial to achieving a low score.

Players will need to exercise their judgement and evaluation skills, assess the risks involved, effectively manage their resources, and engage in strategic thinking throughout the game. The goal of the game is to have the lowest score at the end of the game.

Intial setup

  • The game is played with a custom deck of cards numbered from 3 to 35.
  • Nine cards are randomly removed and the deck is shuffled.
  • At the start of the game, each player is given 11 chips. Chips are worth one negative point each.

Gameplay

  • Each turn, a card is flipped from the deck.
  • The player can either pay one chip to pass saying "No thanks!" or take the card and the chips on it.
  • If they pass, they place one of their chips on the card and move the card to the next player.
  • If the player doesn't have any chips left, then the player must take the card.
  • If the player takes the card, they add it to their score and collect any chips on it. The player then flips the next card from the deck and the process repeats.
  • The game ends when the deck is empty.

Scoring

  • At the end of the game, players accrue points from cards according to their value, but cards in a row only count as a single card with the lowest value. (e.g., a run with cards numbered 30, 29, 28, 27 is worth 27 points).
  • Number of chips are subtracted from the score as they worth one negative point.
  • The player(s) with the lowest number of points win(s) the game.
